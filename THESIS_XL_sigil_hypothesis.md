**Dimension 2 — Curvature (prime family):** Which way the strokes curve. Straight lines map to the 2-family (binary — no curvature, maximum efficiency). **Dimension 3 — Intersection (resonance):** Where strokes cross, overlap, or touch. Each intersection is a resonance node — a point where multiple prime families (stroke-types) converge. More intersections = more intricate lattice at that character's position.

**Dimension 4 — Openness (tier depth):** Whether the character is closed (like O, D, or the Hebrew mem sofit מ) or open (like C, U, or the Hebrew mem מ). Closed characters are complete cycles — Möbius loops that contain their own recursion. Open characters are partial walks — paths that begin and end at different nodes, inviting connection to adjacent characters.

The 4D volume of a character is its total topological content: strokes × curvatures × intersections × openness. This volume determines the character's lattice position, its contribution to the walk, and its interaction with neighboring characters.
